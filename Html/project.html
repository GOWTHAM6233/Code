The Indian stock market is where people buy and sell shares of companies. It has two main exchanges: BSE and NSE. Investors hope to make money if share prices go up. The BSE Sensex and NSE Nifty track top companies' performance. The stock market reflects India's economic health.

1.NSE (National Stock Exchange):

The National Stock Exchange (NSE) is India's largest stock exchange by trading volume, located in Mumbai. It was founded in 1992 to provide a modern, fully electronic trading system. The NSE's key index is the Nifty 50, which tracks the performance of the top 50 companies listed on the exchange. The NSE offers a transparent, efficient, and secure platform for trading various financial instruments, including stocks, bonds, and derivatives.


2.BSE(Bmbay Stock Exchange):

The Bombay Stock Exchange (BSE) is Asia's oldest stock exchange, established in 1875 and located in Mumbai. It provides a platform for buying and selling shares of publicly traded companies. The BSE's key index is the Sensex, which tracks the performance of the top 30 companies listed on the exchange. The BSE facilitates transparent and efficient trading of various financial instruments like stocks, bonds, and mutual funds.

TRADING

Trading is the act of buying and selling financial assets like stocks, bonds, or commodities. People trade to make a profit by purchasing assets at a low price and selling them at a higher price. Trading can be done through stock exchanges, where buyers and sellers meet to exchange these assets. The goal of trading is to take advantage of price changes in the market.

INVESTMENT

Investment is the act of putting money into financial assets, like stocks, bonds, or real estate, with the expectation of earning a profit over time. People invest to grow their wealth and achieve financial goals, such as saving for retirement or buying a house. Unlike trading, which focuses on short-term gains, investment is generally about long-term growth and stability. The goal is to increase the value of the initial amount invested through appreciation, dividends, or interest.
